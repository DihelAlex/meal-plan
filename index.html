Some rules to follow

Here are some universal rules that can help you get where you want.

Rule 1
Drink enough water.

You should drink 33 ml of water for every kg of body weight. If you dont drink enough your body will retain it and this will result in water weight and bloat.

Rule 2
Don't drink your calories.

Avoid sodas, lattes and milkshakes. A milkshake can reach even 600 calories which is an insanely high amount for a beverage

Rule 3
5 hours rule.

Give your body time to break and absorb the nutrients you feed it. You shoud have a 5 hour brake between meals. If you have a busy day check this out

Rule 4
Track & count

Tracking your calories and macros might seem obsessive but you learn a lot about what you eat while doing it. It also prevents you from overeating

Here are some tips to help you.

Tip nr. 1 - Don't overdo it!

Going all in and restrictig yourself for everything you like to eat it's not the way to do it. Instead you need balance. You need to be able to enjoy foods you like, even unhealthy ones but eat them less and improvise with healthier variations.

Remember, 1 slice of pizza won't make you fat just like you wont't lose weight from 1 salad. Sustainability is key and if you are not able to stick to a healthy diet for the rest of your life then you should find other variants.

Tip nr. 2 - Find out what's your calorie intake.

Tip nr. 2 - Find out what's your calorie intake.

You can do this here. You might say that some diets don't work and that is true but the laws of thermodynamics never lie. If your body needs 1800 calories to function and you give it 1700 you WILL lose body fat.

How fast you lose the weight depends of the calorie deficit you are in, however dont go over a 600 calorie deficit because it might backfire. A 500 calorie deficit is ideal because it is sustainable and brings ideal results. One kg of fat has 7000 calories so if you eat 500 calories less daily you will lose 2 kg / month.

After you find out what your calorie intake is you can use apps like Lifesum to track your calories.

Tip nr. 3 - Protein is your friend, fat is not your enemy.

Calories are made up of macronutrients (Protein, Carbohydrates, Fats and Alcohol). 1 gram of protein or carbohydrates has 4 calories while 1 gram of fat has 9 calories and alcohol has 7. Out of these 4 you need the first 3.

Protein is your best friend because it is very satiating and it takes your body more energy to break it down this resulting in a higher thermodynamic effect which will burn more fat. Protein also prevents you from losing muscle mass in the process of losing weight.

While its true that fats break down the easiest, have the highest amout of calories / gram and are not satiating you should't exclude them from your diet. Some sources of healthy fats are eggs, avocados, nuts and cheese. Avoid getting your fats from oils or butter because they are extremely calorie dense and wont fill you up. A tablespoon of olive oil has 120 calories.
